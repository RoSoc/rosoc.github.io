Welcome To Our New Website

It is our pleasure to introduce you to our goals, people, activities and parties. We are doing our best in taking an active part in Edinburgh’s vibrant environment, always trying to engage our members in a wide range of events.

From trips to pub quizzes and social meetings, we make sure there is an ongoing project every two weeks, but we relish long term commitments as well: Charity Shoebox Appeal (a tradition for our community) and a Mentorship Programme, a newly implemented idea.

We are happy to promote the Romanian culture and tradition on an international level; the University of Edinburgh, the world’s most attractive melting pot, is our best chance. Therefore, whether you are Romanian or not, feel free to join our Society, spread the word about our events and rest assured - you will have a wonderful time with us!

Key Milestones

We are proud to have initiated and organized over the time a number of successful activities, in which our members could participate, gain experience and network. We organize both social and cultural events where people from outside Romania can learn more about our culture, position in the world, music and dances.

The 5th Conference of Romanian Students,Professors and Scientists in the UK

The onsite organization and website was made thanks to the committee members (Andrei Franciuc, Ozgur Osman, Radu Constantin Oprescu, Gabriela Brinzan, Ioan Stanciu, Mihai Cimpoiasu, Catalina Predoi, Bogdan Spiridon) and our volunteers (Ionela Iacobescu, Sandra Hapca, Oana Telecan, Adriana Solomon, Andra Zinca). Special thanks to Diana Sisu, one of the main organizers of the conference, which has made this a big success.

Christmas Shoebox Appeal

(In cooperation with LINK Romania and with the kind support of the Accommodation Services, Orthodox Society, different charities). It has become a tradition and for 4 years now, Edinburgh University Romanian Society organizes every autumn a Shoebox appeal. This initiative came in 2009 from Laurentiu Prodan, who was the main organizer of the first three editions. His work was successfully continued this year by Theodor Cojoianu and Laura Ionescu, and this beautiful initiative reached its 4th birthday. More information and photos from the 2009 - 2012 editions you can find attached in Calendar of Events. The 2011 edition had a record number of 27 volunteers out of which 17 were Romanians and 10 were foreign, being one of the most successful events organized.

Lately, we are moving towards organizing events with different societies, so we can grow the diversity and the number of people at our activities. Out of the societies that we have recently collaborated with are the Polish, Bulgarian and Yugoslavic Societies.

About Us

Edinburgh University Romanian Society (E.U. RoSoc) is a EUSA registered society and it was founded in 2007 by a small group of Romanian students: Ana-Elena Sotropa, Eduard Andrei Ciocanescu, Raluca Voinica, Vlad Constantin Dorunga and Laurentiu Prodan. It was a time when the number of Romanian students in Edinburgh was not very large and the main purpose of the activities was to promote and improve the image of Romania to foreigners. We had approximately 90 registered members in 2012 and there were more than 150 Romanian Students in Edinburgh as of 2012. Our events are open to all society members and non-members, visitors and the curious are welcomed! The languages used will be English and Romanian (but translation will be provided where needed), thus supporting our goal of making the above mentioned known to Britain's inhabitants, raising awareness of different activities and facilitating the connection between people mainly involved in academia. Although the description/ motto of the society has changed to: "Sharing the Romanian culture among Edinburgh Students", it still represents the main ideals as before: “The society was made for everyone interested in Romania, Romanian culture and language regardless of the country of origin or other affiliations”.
